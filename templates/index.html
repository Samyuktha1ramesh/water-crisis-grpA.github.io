What is “The Drinkable Book” ?


A portable water purification system that consists of 100 pages of filters


Designed by a group of researchers in the US, in collaboration with a non-profit organisation


Targeted to help communities that suffer from severe sanitation problems


It’s had successful trials in South Africa, Ghana, and Bangladesh.

Each page can potentially filter up to 100 litres of drinking water which is equivalent to 30-days of water supply


Each Drinkable Book can provide a single person with up to four years of clean water!


The filter is created such that it is made of cellulose and impregnated with silver nanoparticles, since silver is an effective antimicrobial and has minimal effect on humans.

To use the book(easy to implement/accessible icon), you rip one of the pages in half, slide it into the filter box, which doubles as a cover for the book, and pour contaminated water through.


The book acts to both educate the user and purify their drinking water—each page has information about sanitation and maintaining water quality. Most filters do not contain this educational component.

After a few minutes, the bacteria (eg. E.Coli) is reduced by 99.999% and is comparable to U.S. tap water.


The books cost just a few dollars to make; each piece of filter paper costs about 10 cents (affordable icon).
